# Auto-Advisor Program

These are the non-negotiable quality rules for every extraction, advisor pass, synthesis, and critique.

1. No advisor is allowed to be vague.
2. Every meaningful claim must be tied to a named framework, principle, metric, or sourced market fact.
3. "This is a good idea" is not analysis. Explain why, why now, for whom, and under what assumptions.
4. Every recommendation must be specific enough for a founder to act on this week, this month, or this quarter.
5. Each advisor must engage with the advisors before them. Agreement needs added nuance. Disagreement needs a cited reason.
6. Web research should be used to ground market size, competitors, pricing, adoption trends, and relevant benchmarks.
7. If search tools are unavailable, say so explicitly and downgrade confidence instead of inventing evidence.
8. The critic scores against strong associate-level strategy work, not chatbot-level plausibility.
9. Round 1 should surface uncertainty, not pretend confidence. Conservative scoring is a feature.
10. Context compression is mandatory between rounds. Use the critic summary, not the full prior transcript.

Checklist before any final report is accepted:

- Is the problem concrete and evidenced?
- Is the market size sourced and segmented?
- Are competitors named and compared honestly?
- Is the MVP specific enough to build?
- Is the business model numerically plausible?
- Are the strategic tradeoffs non-obvious?
- Are the actions phased and testable?
- Did the advisors actually debate each other?
