## 📚 Content by Day

### DAY_1: Sources and Patterns

**Definition of Entrepreneurship (Bygrave & Hofer, 1991):**
> "An entrepreneur is someone who perceives an opportunity and creates an organization to pursue it."

**Two essential components:**
1. **Opportunity Perception** - Identifying gaps, needs, potential
2. **Organizational Creation** - Taking action to build value-capturing structures

**Sources of Business Ideas (Bhidé, 2005 - Inc. 500 Analysis):**
- **71%**: Replicated/modified ideas from previous employment ⭐
- **20%**: Discovered opportunities serendipitously
- **7%**: Built temporary/casual job into business
- **6%**: Wanted something as individual consumer
- **Only 4%**: Engaged in systematic search

**Key Insight:** Industry experience is THE most valuable source. "Insiders" see opportunities outsiders miss.

**Common Opportunity Patterns:**
1. Platform/Marketplace Models ("AirBnB for X", "One stop shop for X")
2. Technology Applications ("Blockchain for X", "AI for anything")
3. On-Demand Service Models
4. Sustainability & Impact Ventures
5. Food & Hospitality Innovation
6. Specialized Services

**B2B vs. B2C Strategic Considerations (Bhide, 2000 - Inc. 5000):**
- Only 10-15% of fastest-growing companies offered consumer goods
- B2B dominated: Higher barriers, predictable revenue, higher LTV
- B2C characteristics: Faster growth potential, viral possibilities, higher CAC

**Geographic Localization:**
- Only ~15% of Inc. 500 operate internationally
- Most success = local/regional focus
- Creates opportunity for geographic adaptation

**The Mentor-Student Paradox:**
- Industry experts (like "Xavier") have best ideas but golden handcuffs prevent pursuit
- Students (like "Maria") have freedom but generate generic ideas
- Solution: Bridge through mentor/advisor relationships (1-2% equity)

### DAY_2: Systematic Evaluation

**PoD (Point of Departure) Rational Analysis Framework:**

Evaluate opportunities across **4 dimensions**:

**1. Market Analysis:**
- **Structure**: Winner-takes-all vs. inherently fragmented
- **Growth**: >10% annual growth creates more opportunity
- **Timing**: Too early | Right now | Too late
  - Too early: Expensive customer education
  - Right now: Inflection point, early adopters ready
  - Too late: Established players, high switching costs
- **Competition**: Direct, indirect, future threats
- **Market Size**:
  - First: "Big enough" ($10M+ revenue potential)
  - Then: Billion+ Euro TAM for venture scale
- **Profitability**: 60%+ margins (software), 40%+ (products)

**2. Team-Market Fit:**
- **Passion**: Can sustain 5-10 years?
- **Deep Domain Knowledge**: Insider vs. outsider perspective
- **Strong Team**: Complementary skills (technical, business, domain)
- **Unfair Advantage**: What do you know/have that others don't?

**3. Customer Dimensions:**
- **Big Budget Potential**: B2B customers with >€100K budgets
- **Top Pain Point**: Must be in customer's top 3 priorities
- **10x Value Proposition**: Incremental improvements insufficient
- **Time to First Sales**: Weeks/months, not years
- **Revenue Ramp**: Can achieve >300% annual growth?

**4. Business Model:**
- **Single vs. Multi-Sided**: Simpler vs. higher defensibility
- **Profitability Margins**: Unit economics must work
- **Capital Efficiency**: Revenue per dollar of capital
- **Fixed Costs**: High fixed costs increase risk

**Tool:** Google ScoreSheet - Quantitatively score each PoD on all criteria

**Common Mistakes:**
1. **Not Top Problem**: Solving pain points #7-9 instead of #1-3
2. **Weak Validation**: Relying on surveys/focus groups instead of contracts/LoIs

### DAY_4-5: Economic Viability

**Unit Economics as Opportunity Filter:**

Opportunities must pass financial viability tests:

**Golden Rule #1: LTV > 3x CAC**
- Lifetime Value must be 3X+ Customer Acquisition Cost
- Below 3x = unsustainable growth

**Golden Rule #2: CAC Payback < 12 Months**
- Time to recover acquisition investment
- Longer payback strains cash flow

**Bottom-Up Revenue Projections:**
- Avoid: "We'll capture 1% of $10B market" (top-down fantasy)
- Use: Actual sales capacity × conversion rates × deal size (bottom-up reality)

**Channel-Opportunity Alignment:**
| Deal Size | Channel | Why |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| $100K+ | Personal Sales | Complexity justifies cost |
| $10K-100K | Telephone/Inside | Balance of touch and efficiency |
| <$10K | Internet/Self-Serve | Scale economics required |

Opportunity evaluation includes: Can this support appropriate go-to-market channel?

## 🔧 Integrated Evaluation Framework

Combine all days' insights into one systematic approach:

### Phase 1: Source Identification (DAY_1)

**Where to Look:**
1. **Employment Experience** (71% of successes)
   - Document pain points in current/past jobs
   - Identify inefficiencies in your industry
   - Leverage insider knowledge

2. **Systematic Search** (4% but learnable)
   - Forbes VC Midas List (follow smart money)
   - Inc 5000 (identify scaling models)
   - Y Combinator RFS (pre-validated demand)
   - Top angel portfolios (pattern recognition)

3. **Geographic Arbitrage**
   - Successful US/European models not in your market
   - Time lag creates windows of opportunity

4. **Personal Pain Points**
   - Problems you face as consumer
   - Workarounds you've built

**Filter Patterns:**
- Does it follow overcrowded pattern? ("Uber for X" without differentiation)
- Is timing right? (Not too early, not too late)
- B2B or B2C? (B2B historically stronger growth)

### Phase 3: Economic Validation (DAY_4-5)

**Test Financial Viability:**

1. **Estimate Unit Economics:**
   ```
   LTV = (Avg Revenue × Gross Margin %) / Churn Rate
   CAC = (Estimated Sales & Marketing Spend) / New Customers

   Does LTV > 3x CAC? ☐ Yes ☐ No
   Can you recover CAC < 12 months? ☐ Yes ☐ No
   ```

2. **Channel Economics:**
   - What's realistic CAC for this opportunity?
   - Does deal size justify required sales channel?
   - Can you afford customer acquisition at scale?

3. **Bottom-Up Revenue Projection:**
   - How many leads can you realistically generate?
   - What's realistic conversion rate?
   - What's average deal size?
   - What's sales cycle time?
   - Calculate: Month-by-month achievable revenue

**If unit economics don't work, opportunity fails regardless of other scores.**

## 💡 Key Decision Points

### Decision 1: Pursue or Pass?

**PURSUE if:**
- ✅ PoD scorecard > 100
- ✅ Team-market fit score > 7/10
- ✅ Top 3 customer pain point
- ✅ LTV > 3x CAC possible
- ✅ CAC payback < 12 months achievable
- ✅ Personal passion for 5-10 years

**PASS if:**
- ❌ Solving problem #7-9 (not top priority)
- ❌ Unit economics fundamentally broken
- ❌ Timing wrong (too early or too late)
- ❌ No unfair advantage vs. established players
- ❌ Team lacks domain expertise
- ❌ Can't achieve first sale within reasonable timeline

### Decision 2: B2B or B2C?

**Choose B2B if:**
- Higher complexity product
- Longer sales cycles acceptable
- Want more predictable revenue
- Building on employment experience
- Higher margins possible

**Choose B2C if:**
- Simpler product
- Viral growth potential
- Fast iteration needed
- Consumer pain point you understand
- Can achieve low CAC via internet channels

### Decision 3: Geographic Strategy

**Start Local if:**
- 85% of Inc. 500 focus single country
- Build strong foundation before international expansion
- Exception: Digital products with no localization barriers

**Geographic Arbitrage (Copycat) if:**
- Proven model exists in other market
- Time lag before original enters your market
- Local adaptations add genuine value
- Speed of execution creates defensible position

## 📖 Real-World Examples

**Employment-Based Opportunities:**
- Most B2B SaaS founders worked in the industry they serve
- Understood pain points intimately
- Had network and credibility
- Example: 71% of Inc. 500 founders

**Systematic Search Success:**
- Y Combinator RFS creates pre-validated opportunities
- Following Forbes Midas List investments reveals trends
- Inc 5000 analysis identifies replicable models

**Geographic Arbitrage:**
- **Zalando** (copied Zappos): €10B market cap independent success
- **CityDeal** (copied Groupon): $100M acquisition
- **Alando** (copied eBay): $43M acquisition

**Failed Due to Timing:**
- Too early: Webvan (1999 grocery delivery, before smartphones)
- Too late: Google+ (social network after Facebook dominance)

**Failed Due to Wrong Problem Priority:**
- Solving pain points #7-9 that customers won't prioritize
- No budget allocation for lower-priority problems

## ✅ Evaluation Checklist

Use this comprehensive checklist for any opportunity:

**Source & Pattern:**
- [ ] Identified through employment experience OR systematic search
- [ ] Not following overcrowded pattern without differentiation
- [ ] B2B vs. B2C decision made based on data
- [ ] Geographic strategy defined (local first vs. arbitrage)

**Rational Analysis:**
- [ ] PoD scorecard completed (score > 100)
- [ ] Market growth >10% annual
- [ ] Timing is "right now" (not too early or late)
- [ ] Market size "big enough" first, billion+ TAM second
- [ ] Team has deep domain knowledge
- [ ] Team has identifiable unfair advantage
- [ ] Solving customer's top 3 pain point (not #7-9)
- [ ] Can demonstrate 10x value proposition

**Economic Validation:**
- [ ] LTV > 3x CAC achievable
- [ ] CAC payback < 12 months possible
- [ ] Gross margins >60% (software) or >40% (products)
- [ ] Deal size aligns with appropriate sales channel
- [ ] Bottom-up revenue projection created
- [ ] First customer achievable within weeks/months

**Team Readiness:**
- [ ] Passion to sustain 5-10 years
- [ ] Can commit to Year 1 founder-led learning
- [ ] Have or can recruit complementary skills
- [ ] Mentor/advisor access if lacking domain expertise

**Final Decision:**
- [ ] All critical checkboxes above checked
- [ ] Discussed with trusted advisors
- [ ] Personal conviction strong
- [ ] Ready to commit
