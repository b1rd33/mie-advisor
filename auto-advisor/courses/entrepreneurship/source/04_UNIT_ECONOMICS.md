## 🎯 Topic Overview

Unit economics determine whether your business model works at the fundamental level—profitability per transaction or customer. This aggregates all concepts, formulas, and frameworks related to unit economics across all days.

**Key Question:** Does your business make money on each customer/transaction, and can it scale profitably?

---

## 📚 Core Concept (Introduced DAY_2, Mastered DAY_4-5)

**Definition:**
Unit economics = Profitability at the individual transaction or customer level

**Critical Principle:**
- Poor unit economics AMPLIFY losses with scale
- Cannot be "fixed" by growth
- Must work before scaling

**Why It Matters:**
- Determines business model viability
- Primary investor evaluation criterion
- Predicts cash flow sustainability
- Reveals scalability potential

## 🔢 The Two Golden Rules (DAY_4-5)

### Rule #1: LTV > 3x CAC

**Customer Lifetime Value must be at least 3X Customer Acquisition Cost**

**Formula:**
```
LTV = (Average Revenue per Customer × Gross Margin %) / Churn Rate

Target: LTV > 3x CAC
```

**Why 3X (not just >1x)?**
- 1x goes to customer acquisition
- 1x goes to operating costs (sales, support, infrastructure)
- 1x goes to profit and growth investment

**Below 3x = Unsustainable growth**

---

### Rule #2: CAC Payback < 12 Months

**Time to recover acquisition investment must be under 12 months**

**Formula:**
```
Payback Period (months) = CAC / (Monthly Recurring Revenue × Gross Margin %)

Target: < 12 months
```

**Why 12 Months?**
- Longer payback strains cash reserves
- Creates dependency on continuous fundraising
- Increases risk of running out of cash before profitability
- Limits growth velocity (cash tied up too long)

## 📊 Component Formulas

### Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

**Full Formula:**
```
LTV = (Average Revenue per Customer × Gross Margin %) / Churn Rate
```

**Alternative Formula (for cohort analysis):**
```
LTV = Σ(Revenue in Period × Gross Margin %) for all periods customer remains
```

**Example Calculation:**
```
SaaS Product:
- Monthly subscription: $1,000
- Annual revenue per customer: $12,000
- Gross margin: 80%
- Annual churn: 20%

LTV = ($12,000 × 80%) / 20% = $48,000
```

---

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Formula:**
```
CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Spend / Number of New Customers Acquired
```

**Include in S&M Spend:**
- Salaries (sales, marketing, customer success)
- Advertising and paid media
- Marketing tools and software
- Events and conferences
- Content creation costs
- Agency/contractor fees

**Exclude:**
- Product development
- General overhead
- Non-acquisition customer success

**Example Calculation:**
```
- Monthly S&M spend: $50,000
- New customers acquired: 20
CAC = $50,000 / 20 = $2,500
```

---

### Churn Rate

**Formula:**
```
Monthly Churn Rate = Customers Lost in Month / Total Customers at Start of Month

Annual Churn Rate = 1 - (1 - Monthly Churn)^12
```

**Example:**
```
- Start of month: 100 customers
- Lost during month: 3 customers
Monthly Churn = 3 / 100 = 3% = 0.03

Annual Churn = 1 - (1 - 0.03)^12 = 1 - 0.6938 = 30.62%
```

**Lower churn = Higher LTV**

---

### Gross Margin

**Formula:**
```
Gross Margin % = (Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold) / Revenue × 100
```

**What to Include in COGS:**
- Direct product delivery costs
- Hosting/infrastructure (for SaaS)
- Payment processing fees
- Customer support (sometimes)

**What to Exclude:**
- Sales & marketing
- R&D / product development
- General & administrative

**Targets:**
- SaaS: 70-90% gross margin
- E-commerce: 30-50% gross margin
- Marketplace: 60-80% gross margin

## 💰 SaaS Pricing & Economics (DAY_4 - Christoph Janz Framework)

**Pricing Tier Impact:**

| Monthly Price | Annual ARR | Typical CAC | Approx CLV | LTV/CAC | Payback | Churn |
|---------------|------------|-------------|------------|---------|---------|-------|
| Low ($100) | $1,200 | $300 | $1,200 | 4x | 3-6 mo | High (30%+) |
| Mid ($1K) | $12K | $3K | $36K | 12x | 6-12 mo | Medium (15-20%) |
| High ($8K+) | $96K+ | $24K | $1M+ | 40x+ | 12-18 mo | Low (<10%) |

**Key Insights:**
1. **Higher pricing = Lower churn** (products become "stickier")
2. **Higher pricing = Can afford higher CAC** (more room for sales investment)
3. **Enterprise sales justify 18-month payback** (complex, high-value)
4. **SMB/self-serve needs <6-month payback** (competitive, volatile)

**Strategic Implication:**
Don't under-price. Higher pricing often indicates higher value and creates better economics.

## 🛍️ E-Commerce & Retail Unit Economics (DAY_4)

### Branded Goods Retailer Model

**Example:**
- Purchase price: €70
- Retail price: €120
- Shipping: €5
- On-site conversion: 1%

**Calculation:**
```
Margin per sale = €120 - €70 - €5 = €45
100 clicks → 1 sale → €45 revenue
Max profitable CPC = €45 / 100 = €0.45 (break-even)
Target CPC = €0.15 (to maintain 67% profit margin)
```

### Direct-to-Consumer (DTC) Model

**Example:**
- Fully landed cost: €15
- Retail price: €75
- Shipping: €5
- On-site conversion: 1%

**Calculation:**
```
Margin per sale = €75 - €15 - €5 = €55
100 clicks → 1 sale → €55 revenue
Max profitable CPC = €55 / 100 = €0.55
Target CPC = €0.18
```

**Critical Insight:**
DTC economics (€55 margin) superior to branded retail (€45 margin)
- Allows 22% higher customer acquisition spend
- Demonstrates why brands shift to DTC

## 📈 Unit Economics by Business Model

### SaaS (Software as a Service)

**Ideal Metrics:**
- LTV/CAC: 3x minimum, 5x+ excellent
- Payback: <12 months
- Gross Margin: 75-85%
- Annual Churn: <10% (enterprise), <25% (SMB)
- MRR Growth: 10-20%/month early stage

**Example (Good):**
```
Monthly subscription: $500
Annual revenue: $6,000
Gross margin: 80%
Annual churn: 15%
CAC: $2,000

LTV = ($6,000 × 80%) / 15% = $32,000
LTV/CAC = $32,000 / $2,000 = 16x ✅
Payback = $2,000 / ($500 × 80%) = 5 months ✅
```

### E-Commerce

**Ideal Metrics:**
- LTV/CAC: 3x minimum
- Payback: <6 months (faster due to lower margins)
- Gross Margin: 40-60%
- Repeat purchase rate: >30%
- Average orders per customer: 3-5

**Example (Marginal):**
```
Average order value: $75
Gross margin: 50%
Repeat purchase rate: 30%/year
Lifetime purchases: 1.43
CAC: $30

LTV = $75 × 1.43 × 50% = $53.63
LTV/CAC = $53.63 / $30 = 1.79x ❌ (below 3x)
Payback = ~8 months ✅

Verdict: CAC too high OR need higher repeat rates/margins
```

### Marketplace/Platform

**Ideal Metrics:**
- LTV/CAC: 4x+ (need buffer for two-sided costs)
- Payback: <12 months
- Take rate: 15-25% of transaction value
- Gross margin: 60-80% (mostly fixed costs after platform built)
- Network effects: Accelerating with scale

**Complexity:** Must achieve positive unit economics on EACH side

**Example:**
```
Average transaction: $200
Take rate: 20%
Revenue per transaction: $40
Cost to facilitate: $5
Margin per transaction: $35
Transactions per customer lifetime: 10
CAC (supply side): $150
CAC (demand side): $100

LTV (demand side) = 10 × $35 = $350
Combined CAC = $250
LTV/CAC = $350 / $250 = 1.4x ❌

Needs improvement: Higher transaction frequency or lower CAC
```

## 🔧 Improving Unit Economics

### Improving LTV

**Strategies:**

1. **Increase Average Revenue:**
   - Upselling to higher plans
   - Cross-selling additional products
   - Usage-based pricing (grows with customer)

2. **Increase Gross Margin:**
   - Negotiate better supplier terms
   - Reduce COGS through efficiency
   - Automate delivery/support

3. **Reduce Churn:**
   - Improve product value (features customers need)
   - Better customer success and onboarding
   - Annual contracts vs. monthly
   - Increase switching costs (integrations, data lock-in)

**Impact Example:**
```
Current: LTV = ($10K revenue × 70% margin) / 25% churn = $28K
Reduce churn to 15%: LTV = ($10K × 70%) / 15% = $46.67K
Improvement: +67% LTV
```

### Reducing CAC

**Strategies:**

1. **Optimize Acquisition Channels:**
   - Test and measure all channels
   - Double down on lowest-CAC channels
   - Cut underperforming channels

2. **Improve Conversion Rates:**
   - Better landing pages
   - Stronger value proposition
   - Reduced friction in signup
   - Social proof and trust signals

3. **Leverage Organic/Viral Growth:**
   - Referral programs
   - Word-of-mouth amplification
   - Content marketing / SEO
   - Product-led growth

4. **Increase Sales Efficiency:**
   - Better lead qualification
   - Shorter sales cycles
   - Higher close rates
   - Automate where possible

**Impact Example:**
```
Current: CAC = $5,000
Improve conversion 20% → 24%: CAC = $4,167
Improvement: -17% CAC
```

### Reducing Payback Period

**Strategies:**

1. **Annual Contracts:**
   - Get 12 months revenue upfront
   - Instant payback if CAC < annual revenue
   - Offer discount for annual (10-20%) still worth it

2. **Increase Pricing:**
   - More revenue per month = faster payback
   - Test willingness to pay higher prices

3. **Reduce CAC:**
   - As above strategies
   - Faster payback even with same LTV

**Impact Example:**
```
Current:
- Monthly revenue: $500
- CAC: $6,000
- Payback: 12 months

Switch to annual contracts ($5,000/year upfront):
- Payback: Immediate (if margin > CAC)
- Or payback: ~14 months at 85% margin = $4,250 first payment
```

## 📉 Warning Signs (Bad Unit Economics)

**RED FLAGS:**

1. **LTV/CAC < 3x**
   - Growth will burn cash
   - Unsustainable without continuous fundraising
   - Indicates fundamental business model problem

2. **Payback > 18 months**
   - Even for enterprise, this is concerning
   - Ties up too much capital too long
   - Limits growth velocity

3. **High Churn (>30% annual for SaaS)**
   - Product not delivering value
   - Wrong target customer
   - LTV calculations become meaningless

4. **Negative Gross Margin**
   - Losing money on each sale
   - Scale makes it WORSE, not better
   - Immediate red flag

5. **CAC Increasing with Scale**
   - Should decrease with learning
   - If increasing: saturation of best channels
   - Indicates limited market or poor product-market fit

6. **LTV Decreasing Over Time**
   - Early customers ≠ typical customers
   - Later cohorts churning faster
   - Product value declining

**If You See These:**
- STOP scaling
- Fix unit economics FIRST
- Scaling bad economics = faster death

## 💡 Real-World Examples

**Zipcar (DAY_4 Case):**
- Car-sharing pioneer
- Challenge: High fixed costs per vehicle
- Required high utilization rates for profitability
- Unit economics: Revenue per car vs. cost per car
- Learning: Must model unit economics BEFORE scaling fleet

**SaaS Success Pattern:**
- $1K+/month pricing
- 15% annual churn
- $3K CAC
- Result: LTV/CAC of 10-15x
- Sustainable, profitable growth

**E-Commerce Struggle:**
- $50 average order
- 40% margin = $20
- $25 CAC
- 1.5 lifetime orders
- LTV = $30, LTV/CAC = 1.2x ❌
- Result: Can't scale profitably

**Marketplace Scaling:**
- Early: Subsidize both sides (negative unit economics)
- Growth: One side becomes profitable
- Maturity: Both sides profitable, network effects kick in
- Result: Patience required, but defensible once achieved

## ✅ Unit Economics Checklist

**For Any Business Model:**

**Calculate:**
- [ ] Average revenue per customer (monthly/annual)
- [ ] Gross margin %
- [ ] Churn rate (monthly and annual)
- [ ] LTV using formula
- [ ] Total S&M spend
- [ ] Number of new customers
- [ ] CAC using formula
- [ ] LTV/CAC ratio
- [ ] Payback period in months

**Evaluate:**
- [ ] Is LTV > 3x CAC? (minimum requirement)
- [ ] Is payback < 12 months? (<6 for e-commerce)
- [ ] Is gross margin healthy? (>70% SaaS, >40% e-commerce)
- [ ] Is churn acceptable? (<20% annual SaaS)
- [ ] Do economics improve with scale?
- [ ] Can you forecast 12-24 months out?

**Act:**
- [ ] If metrics fail: STOP scaling, fix economics
- [ ] If metrics pass: Document and track cohorts
- [ ] Create improvement plan for each metric
- [ ] Set targets for next quarter
- [ ] Review monthly, don't let metrics drift
